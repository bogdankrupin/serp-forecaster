When will the next Google update land?

The forecast is built on the confirmed core updates in Google's Search Status Dashboard: we measure cadence (intervals between launches) and seasonality (which months Google has historically shipped updates in), then project forward from the last known launch.

This isn't a prophecy. Google doesn't publish a schedule and openly says it doesn't confirm every update. The tool shows a statistically likely window, not a date — treat it as a monitoring cue, not a deadline.

How it's computed: we take the launch dates of core updates and measure the intervals between them. Window center = last date + median of the last 6 intervals; window bounds = the 25th and 75th percentiles of those intervals. The seasonal dial shows which months hosted updates most often — when cadence and season line up, confidence is higher.
